# Spacecraft Buses

A spacecraft bus is the supporting structure and infrastructure that carries and operates the payload. It provides power, attitude control, communications, thermal management, and computing. Standardized bus platforms reduce cost and risk by reusing proven designs.

---

## Bus vs. Payload

| Component | Bus | Payload |
|-----------|-----|---------|
| What it does | Keeps spacecraft alive | Accomplishes the mission |
| Examples | EPS, ADCS, OBC, Comms | Camera, radar, science instrument |
| Lifetime requirement | Full mission | Operational period |
| Redundancy | Required | Mission-dependent |

## Government/Agency Bus Standards

### NASA

NASA programs often use mission-unique buses but reuse heritage:

- **LRO (Lunar Reconnaissance Orbiter)**: derived from GSFC heritage
- **MAVEN, MRO**: designed specifically for Mars orbit insertion ΔV budget
- **GOES-R**: large GEO weather satellite bus

### ESA

- **Eurostar Bus**: standard for commercial GEO missions
- **PRIMA**: medium-class satellite platform for science missions

### CubeSat Standard

See [CubeSat Hardware](./cubesat-hardware) for the 1U–12U standard form factors used by universities and commercial small-sat operators.

## Bus Subsystems

### Structure

- Primary structure: aluminum or CFRP (carbon fiber reinforced polymer)
- Must survive launch loads (typically 20g quasi-static, 100–2000 Hz random vibration)
- Stiffness requirements: first mode > launch vehicle minimum (typically > 35 Hz)

### Thermal Control

See [Thermal Vacuum](./thermal-vacuum) for passive/active thermal control details.

### Propulsion

- Options: cold gas, monoprop, biprop, electric
- Sized to ΔV budget: orbit raise, stationkeeping, deorbit
- Propellant fraction can dominate bus mass in high-ΔV missions

### Power

See [Power Systems](./power-systems) for EPS architecture.

### Communications

- Typically S-band TT&C (command/telemetry)
- High-rate science/payload downlink on X-band or Ka-band
- Telemetry rates: 1 kbps – 1+ Gbps depending on band and antenna

## Bus Performance Parameters

When selecting or designing a bus, these are the key traded parameters:

| Parameter | How to Size It |
|-----------|---------------|
| Power | Payload power + bus overhead + margin (30%) |
| Mass | Launch vehicle capability − payload mass |
| Volume | Payload envelope + bus equipment |
| Pointing | Payload requirements drive ADCS accuracy spec |
| Data rate | Downlink duty cycle × science data rate |
| Lifetime | Mission requirement + consumables (propellant, battery) |
