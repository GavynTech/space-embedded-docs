# Sensors & Actuators

A spacecraft's sensors measure its environment and state; its actuators change that state. Together they close the control loop that keeps the vehicle pointed, powered, and operating correctly.

---

## Attitude Sensors

### Sun Sensors

Determine the direction to the Sun. Essential for initial attitude acquisition and power generation:

| Type | Accuracy | Notes |
|------|----------|-------|
| Coarse (analog) | ±10–20° | Simple photodiodes, always-on |
| Fine (digital) | ±0.1–1° | Active pixel sensor, higher power |

### Magnetometer

Measures Earth's magnetic field vector. Combined with a magnetic field model (IGRF) to determine attitude:

- 3-axis fluxgate or AMR magnetometer
- Accuracy: 10–100 nT typically
- Low power, always-on sensor

### Star Tracker

Photographs the star field, matches against a catalog, outputs precise inertial attitude:

- Accuracy: 1–30 arcseconds
- Requires ~0.5–5W, needs clear FOV
- Cannot operate near bright objects (Sun, Moon, Earth limb)
- Gold standard for fine pointing

### IMU (Gyroscopes)

Measures angular rate. Integrated to propagate attitude between sensor updates:

| Type | Drift Rate | Notes |
|------|-----------|-------|
| MEMS gyro | 1–100°/hr | Low cost, higher noise |
| Fiber optic gyro | 0.001–0.1°/hr | High accuracy, heavy |
| Hemispherical resonator gyro | 0.001°/hr | Heritage on deep space missions |

### GPS / GNSS Receiver

Provides position, velocity, and precise time in LEO:

- Position: ±5–50 m
- Velocity: ±0.1 m/s
- Time: ±50 ns (1-PPS output)

## Attitude Actuators

### Reaction Wheels

Store angular momentum by spinning. Torque the spacecraft by changing spin rate:

```c
// Wheel torque command
// T_wheel = -T_desired (reaction)
void rw_set_torque_nm(uint8_t axis, float torque_nm) {
    int16_t cmd = (int16_t)(torque_nm / TORQUE_SCALE);
    can_send(RW_CAN_ID + axis, &cmd, sizeof(cmd));
}
```

- 3 wheels minimum (one per axis), 4 for redundancy
- Must be periodically desaturated using magnetorquers

### Magnetorquers

Coils that interact with Earth's magnetic field to generate torque. Cannot command torque along the magnetic field vector:

- Used for attitude acquisition, wheel desaturation
- Very low power, no moving parts

### Thrusters

Cold gas, monoprop (hydrazine), biprop, or electric (ion, Hall effect):

| Type | Isp | Thrust | Use |
|------|-----|--------|-----|
| Cold gas (N₂) | 50–70 s | mN | CubeSat attitude |
| Monoprop (hydrazine) | 220 s | 0.5–22 N | Orbit correction |
| Biprop (MMH/NTO) | 310 s | 5–500 N | Major burns |
| Hall thruster (ion) | 1500–3000 s | 0.01–5 N | Low thrust, high ΔV |

## Environmental Sensors

| Sensor | Measurement | Interface |
|--------|-------------|-----------|
| Thermistor | Temperature | Analog (ADC) |
| Current sensor | Bus current | I2C / SPI |
| Voltage sensor | Bus voltage | I2C / SPI |
| Radiation monitor | Total ionizing dose | SPI / UART |
| Pressure sensor | Propellant tank | Analog / I2C |

---

## Sensor Interfaces

Most spacecraft sensors communicate over:

| Interface | Speed | Notes |
|-----------|-------|-------|
| I2C | 100kbps – 1Mbps | Simple, multi-drop, short distance |
| SPI | 1–50Mbps | Full duplex, single master |
| UART/RS-422 | 9.6kbps – 10Mbps | Long cable runs |
| CAN | 1Mbps | Fault-tolerant bus, ECSS-E-ST-50-15 |
| SpaceWire | 2–400Mbps | LVDS, point-to-point, space standard |
| MIL-STD-1553 | 1Mbps | Avionics heritage, highly reliable |

---

## Redundancy for Critical Sensors

All mission-critical sensors should be redundant:

- Dual IMUs with cross-check voting
- Dual sun sensors per axis
- Primary + backup GPS receiver
- Sensor failure detected by sanity checks (physics bounds), not just comms errors
