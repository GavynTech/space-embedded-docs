# CubeSat Hardware

CubeSats are standardized small satellites built in units (U) of 10×10×10 cm. A 1U weighs ~1.33 kg maximum. The CubeSat standard (originally from Cal Poly/Stanford, now governed by the CubeSat Design Specification) enables low-cost access to orbit using commercial off-the-shelf (COTS) and semi-custom hardware.

## Standard Subsystems

### OBC (On-Board Computer)

Runs flight software. Common CubeSat OBCs:

| OBC | CPU | Clock | Flash | SRAM | Radiation |
|-----|-----|-------|-------|------|-----------|
| Gomspace NanoMind A3200 | AVR32 | 66 MHz | 32 MB | 32 MB | Commercial |
| ISIS iOBC | ARM9 | 400 MHz | 1 GB SD | 64 MB | Commercial |
| Endurosat OBC II | STM32H7 | 400 MHz | 4 MB | 1 MB | Commercial |
| Xiphos Q7S | Xilinx Zynq | 667 MHz | 512 MB | 1 GB | Space-grade |

### EPS (Electrical Power System)

- Solar panels (deployable or body-mounted) → Battery → Power bus
- Typical bus voltages: 3.3V, 5V, 12V unregulated
- Battery: Li-Ion or LiPo, 20–80 Wh typical
- Power budget per subsystem enforced by load switches

### ADCS (Attitude Determination and Control)

CubeSat-grade options:

| Type | Accuracy | Power |
|------|----------|-------|
| Magnetorquers only | ±5° | < 1W |
| Magnetorquers + momentum wheel | ±1° | 1–3W |
| Reaction wheels + sun sensors + star tracker | < 0.1° | 3–8W |

### Comms

| Link | Band | Rate |
|------|------|------|
| UHF beacon | 435–438 MHz | 9.6–38.4 kbps |
| UHF/VHF TT&C | 145/435 MHz | 9.6 kbps |
| S-band downlink | 2.4 GHz | 1–100 Mbps |
| X-band downlink | 8–10 GHz | 100 Mbps–1 Gbps |

## PC/104 and CubeSat Bus Standards

Most CubeSat electronics use the **PC/104** form factor and stacking connector. Boards stack vertically with a 104-pin bus carrying:

- Power rails (3.3V, 5V, 12V, unregulated battery)
- I2C (house bus)
- SPI
- UART
- GPIO

Newer platforms use the **CubeSat Space Protocol (CSP)** over CAN or I2C for inter-subsystem communication.

---

## Separation System

The CubeSat is deployed from a **P-POD (Poly Picosatellite Orbital Deployer)** or equivalent. A spring mechanism ejects the satellite when the P-POD door opens. This creates a mechanical shock event the hardware must survive.

**Deployment inhibit requirement**: CubeSats must not transmit RF for at least 30 minutes after deployment (per radio regulations). A deployment switch (D-switch) enforces this in hardware.

## Design Constraints

| Constraint | Typical Limit |
|-----------|--------------|
| Mass | Form factor maximum |
| Power (average) | Solar input − battery margin |
| Peak power | Battery capacity / duty cycle |
| Volume for payload | Total − all subsystems |
| Orbit lifetime (drag) | > 25 years post-mission deorbit required |
| EMC | No interference with launch vehicle or co-passengers |
