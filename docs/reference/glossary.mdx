# Aerospace Glossary

Aerospace has more acronyms per sentence than any other field. This is the reference.

---

## A

**ADCS** — Attitude Determination and Control System. The subsystem responsible for determining the spacecraft's orientation and commanding actuators to achieve a desired attitude.

**AGC** — Automatic Gain Control. Receiver circuit that adjusts signal amplification based on incoming signal strength.

**AIT** — Assembly, Integration, and Test. The process of assembling spacecraft components, integrating subsystems, and testing the complete system.

**AOS** — Acquisition of Signal. The moment a ground station establishes communication with a spacecraft.

**AOCS** — Attitude and Orbit Control System. European term for what Americans call ADCS, sometimes extended to include orbit maneuver capability.

---

## B

**Bdot** — Rate-based detumble control law. Applies magnetic torque proportional to the rate of change of the measured magnetic field. Simple, reliable, requires only a magnetometer and magnetorquers. Used as initial detumble mode after deployment.

**BOL** — Beginning of Life. Performance specifications at time of launch, before any degradation.

**BSS** — Block Started by Symbol. Memory section for zero-initialized global and static variables.

---

## C

**CCSDS** — Consultative Committee for Space Data Systems. International organization that defines space communication standards. CCSDS packets are the standard format for spacecraft command and telemetry.

**CDH** — Command and Data Handling. The subsystem responsible for processing commands and managing onboard data.

**CEI** — Contract End Item. A deliverable defined in a government contract.

**CMG** — Control Moment Gyroscope. High-torque attitude actuator used on large spacecraft (ISS). Distinct from reaction wheels.

**CoG / CoM** — Center of Gravity / Center of Mass.

---

## D

**DAL** — Development Assurance Level. DO-178C classification (A–E) indicating required rigor of software development based on severity of failure consequence.

**DRAM** — Dynamic RAM. Volatile memory requiring periodic refresh. More susceptible to SEUs than SRAM. Rarely used in space for critical data.

**DSN** — Deep Space Network. NASA's network of large ground antennas supporting interplanetary missions.

---

## E

**ECSS** — European Cooperation for Space Standardization. Body that produces space standards including ECSS-E-ST-40 for software.

**EDAC** — Error Detection and Correction. Hardware implementation of ECC for memory. Often used interchangeably with ECC.

**ECI** — Earth Centered Inertial. Coordinate frame with origin at Earth's center, non-rotating, X toward vernal equinox.

**ECEF** — Earth Centered Earth Fixed. Rotating with Earth. Used for GPS and ground station coordinates.

**EKF** — Extended Kalman Filter. Nonlinear extension of the Kalman filter, used for attitude and orbit estimation.

**EOL** — End of Life. Performance specifications at end of mission.

**EPS** — Electrical Power System. Subsystem managing solar panels, batteries, and power distribution.

---

## F

**FDIR** — Fault Detection, Isolation, and Recovery. The autonomous fault management system.

**FEC** — Forward Error Correction. Error correction codes applied to transmitted data so the receiver can correct errors without retransmission.

**FOV** — Field of View.

**FSW / FSW** — Flight Software.

---

## G

**GCR** — Galactic Cosmic Ray. High-energy particles from outside the solar system.

**GEO** — Geostationary Earth Orbit. ~35,786 km altitude, orbital period = 24 hours, appears stationary from ground.

**GOES** — Geostationary Operational Environmental Satellite. NOAA weather satellites.

**GPS** — Global Positioning System. Also refers to the time system: GPS time = TAI − 19 seconds, does not include leap seconds.

---

## H

**HGA** — High Gain Antenna. Directional antenna providing high data rate but narrow beam — must be pointed.

**HIL / HITL** — Hardware-in-the-Loop testing. Test configuration where real hardware is included in the test loop.

**HSM** — Hierarchical State Machine. State machine where states can have sub-states, with entry/exit/transition actions.

**HK / Housekeeping** — Periodic telemetry of spacecraft health data (temperatures, voltages, currents, mode).

---

## I

**IMU** — Inertial Measurement Unit. Sensor combining gyroscopes and accelerometers.

**IPC** — Inter-Process Communication. Mechanisms for tasks/processes to share data (queues, semaphores, shared memory).

**ISL** — Inter-Satellite Link. Communication link between satellites in a constellation.

**ISR** — Interrupt Service Routine. Code that executes in response to a hardware interrupt.

---

## K

**Keplerian Elements** — Six orbital elements that uniquely define an orbit: semi-major axis (a), eccentricity (e), inclination (i), RAAN (Ω), argument of periapsis (ω), true anomaly (ν).

---

## L

**LEO** — Low Earth Orbit. Roughly 200–2,000 km altitude.

**LOS** — Loss of Signal. When a ground station loses contact with a spacecraft.

**LV** — Launch Vehicle.

**LVLH** — Local Vertical Local Horizontal. Spacecraft-centered coordinate frame where Z points toward Earth center, X along velocity vector.

---

## M

**MEO** — Medium Earth Orbit. 2,000–35,786 km.

**MET** — Mission Elapsed Time. Time since spacecraft separation from launch vehicle.

**MMIO** — Memory-Mapped I/O. Hardware registers addressed as memory locations.

**MTQ / MTR** — Magnetorquer. Coil that generates a magnetic dipole, used with Earth's magnetic field for attitude control.

---

## N

**NADIR** — Direction pointing toward Earth's center from the spacecraft.

**NAND / NOR flash** — Non-volatile storage technology. NAND is denser (used for data storage), NOR is byte-addressable (used for code execution).

---

## O

**OBC** — On-Board Computer. The main flight computer.

**OSAL** — OS Abstraction Layer. NASA cFS component that provides RTOS-independent API.

**OBSW** — On-Board Software. European term for flight software.

---

## P

**PID** — Proportional-Integral-Derivative. Common control law.

**PSP** — Platform Support Package. NASA cFS component providing board-specific abstraction.

**PTT** — Pass-Through Telemetry. Raw data relayed without processing.

---

## Q

**Quaternion** — 4-element rotation representation. Preferred for spacecraft attitude because it has no singularities.

---

## R

**RAAN** — Right Ascension of Ascending Node. Orbital element defining the orientation of the orbital plane relative to the vernal equinox.

**RAD750** — Radiation-hardened PowerPC processor from BAE Systems. The most widely used space processor for high-reliability missions.

**RW** — Reaction Wheel. Electric motor with a flywheel. Stores angular momentum to control spacecraft attitude.

**RTOS** — Real-Time Operating System.

---

## S

**SAA** — South Atlantic Anomaly. Region over the South Atlantic where the inner Van Allen belt dips closest to Earth's surface. Highest SEU rates for LEO spacecraft.

**SBand** — Frequency band (2–4 GHz). Commonly used for spacecraft telemetry and command.

**SB** — Software Bus (in cFS context).

**SEE** — Single Event Effect. Radiation-induced effect in electronics.

**SEL** — Single Event Latchup. Destructive radiation effect.

**SEU** — Single Event Upset. Non-destructive bit flip from radiation.

**SGP4** — Simplified General Perturbations model 4. Standard algorithm for propagating satellite TLEs.

**SRAM** — Static RAM. Volatile, fast memory used for program data. More radiation-tolerant than DRAM.

**SSO** — Sun-Synchronous Orbit. Polar orbit precessing at the same rate the Earth orbits the Sun — the spacecraft always crosses the equator at the same local solar time.

---

## T

**TAI** — International Atomic Time. Continuous time scale, no leap seconds.

**TBL** — Table (in cFS context). Configurable data structure updatable via uplink.

**TID** — Total Ionizing Dose. Cumulative radiation damage over mission life.

**TLE** — Two-Line Element set. Standardized format for encoding Keplerian orbital elements.

**TMR** — Triple Modular Redundancy.

**TT&C** — Telemetry, Tracking, and Command.

---

## U

**UHF** — Ultra High Frequency (300 MHz – 3 GHz). Used for backup command uplink, CubeSat communications.

**UTC** — Coordinated Universal Time. Civil time standard, includes leap seconds.

---

## V

**VSCMG** — Variable Speed Control Moment Gyroscope.

**VxWorks** — Commercial RTOS by Wind River, widely used in avionics and spacecraft.

---

## W

**WCET** — Worst-Case Execution Time. Maximum time a function or task can take to execute, used in real-time scheduling analysis.

**WMM** — World Magnetic Model. NOAA/BGS model of Earth's magnetic field. Used by ADCS software to determine expected magnetometer reading given position and time.

---

## X

**Xband** — Frequency band (8–12 GHz). Used for high-rate data downlink from imaging satellites.
