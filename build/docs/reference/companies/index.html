Space Companies

The landscape of companies building spacecraft software infrastructure — what they build, what problems they solve, and what kind of engineering they hire for.

Launch Vehicles​

Companies that build rockets. Their FSW problems: flight control, engine management, vehicle health monitoring, ground software.

Firefly Aerospace (Austin, TX)​

Alpha and Beta launch vehicles targeting small-to-medium payloads. Growing from a near-failure to successful orbital launches. FSW stack includes flight computer software, telemetry systems, and ground software.

Software focus: Flight control software, avionics FSW, ground systems, mission operations.

Relativity Space (Long Beach, CA)​

3D-printed launch vehicles (Terran 1, Terran R). Novel manufacturing but conventional avionics challenges — plus the software to control metal 3D printers at aerospace scale.

Software focus: Flight software, GNC (guidance, navigation, control), manufacturing software (Python/C++ for print process control).

Rocket Lab (Long Beach, CA / Auckland, NZ)​

Electron (small launch, proven) and Neutron (medium, in development). First-stage reuse via helicopter catch. Very active launch cadence.

Software focus: Flight software (C/C++), GNC, avionics, ground systems, mission operations. Well-known for solid engineering culture.

SpaceX (Hawthorne, CA)​

Falcon 9, Falcon Heavy, Starship. Also Starlink constellation (4,000+ satellites). Most prolific launch provider in the world.

Software focus: FSW, GNC, Starlink operations software, ground systems. Uses C++ heavily. Competitive, fast-paced.

Satellites & Constellations​

Spire Global (San Francisco, CA)​

Constellation of 100+ small satellites (3U/6U CubeSats) providing weather, maritime, and aviation data. One of the largest CubeSat operators.

Software focus: Flight software for small sats, ground software, data pipeline. Good entry point for FSW with CubeSat-scale complexity.

Planet Labs (San Francisco, CA)​

World's largest constellation of Earth observation satellites (Doves, SuperDoves). Daily imaging of the entire Earth.

Software focus: FSW, operations automation, data processing pipelines, image processing. Mix of embedded and backend engineering.

Axelspace (Tokyo, Japan)​

Small satellite manufacturer and Earth observation operator (GRUS constellation). Growing presence in commercial small sat market.

Software focus: FSW development, satellite operations. Japanese aerospace market entry point.

Satellogic (Buenos Aires / New York)​

High-resolution, low-cost Earth observation constellation. Focused on making satellite imagery affordable.

Software focus: FSW, mission operations, data products.

Maxar Technologies (Westminster, CO)​

Operator of WorldView imaging satellites. Also builds satellites (buses, instruments) for government and commercial customers.

Software focus: FSW for large, complex imaging satellites. More traditional aerospace culture.

Communications​

OneWeb / Eutelsat (London / Paris)​

LEO broadband constellation (648 satellites). Starlink competitor.

Software focus: Constellation management software, ground systems, link management.

Iridium (McLean, VA)​

66-satellite LEO constellation providing global voice and data. The original LEO constellation, relaunched in 2019.

Software focus: Network operations, satellite control software.

Ground / JSAT​

SKY Perfect JSAT (Tokyo, Japan)​

Japan's dominant satellite operator. GEO communications satellites, growing into LEO operations. Also operating the "Space Compass" LEO infrastructure initiative.

Software focus: Satellite operations, ground segment software, network management.

Defense & Traditional Aerospace​

Northrop Grumman​

James Webb Space Telescope (bus), MEV satellite servicing vehicles, defense systems.

Lockheed Martin (Space division, Denver, CO)​

Orion crew capsule, GPS III, A2100 bus. Large program culture.

Raytheon Intelligence & Space​

Classified and commercial satellite programs.

L3Harris Technologies​

Communications satellites, missile warning systems.

Tools & Services​

Ansys (formerly AGI) — Systems Tool Kit (STK)​

The standard tool for mission analysis, coverage analysis, and link budget calculations. Not hardware, but used universally.

Mathworks — Matlab/Simulink​

GNC algorithm development, auto-code generation (Embedded Coder). ADCS algorithms often prototyped here before C++ implementation.

Wind River — VxWorks​

The dominant commercial RTOS for avionics and space. VxWorks 653 is DO-178C certifiable.

What They Actually Hire For​

RoleWhat You Need
Flight Software EngineerC/C++, RTOS (FreeRTOS/RTEMS/VxWorks), cFS, embedded debugging
GNC EngineerOrbital mechanics, Kalman filtering, MATLAB/Simulink, C++ implementation
Ground Software EngineerPython, C++, distributed systems, real-time data pipelines
Systems EngineerSystems architecture, requirements, interface definition, verification
FPGA EngineerVHDL/Verilog or HLS, radiation-hardened FPGAs, high-speed interfaces

The most direct path from this reference to employment is Flight Software Engineer — the role that directly implements what this reference teaches.
